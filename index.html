Curriculum Vitae

I am an ETH Zurich Postdoctoral Fellow in the International Relations and Data Science Lab. I received my PhD in Political Science from the University of California, Berkeley, and hold an MPA/ID from the Harvard Kennedy School. I am also a Research Associate at the Center on the Politics of Development.

I am a political scientist working in the politics of development, with research spanning comparative politics and political behavior. My scholarship examines how social hierarchies—especially gender and race—shape the ways individuals engage institutions under conditions of inequality. Across projects, I analyze institutional choice, rights enforcement, and immigrant incorporation, asking when formal reforms translate into meaningful behavioral and political change.

Methodologically, I combine fieldwork with administrative and archival data, surveys, and experimental designs. My work advances precise measures of relational power to better understand how policy reforms interact with entrenched social structures to produce behavior. Together, these projects contribute to broader debates on governance, inequality, institutional access and accountability in developing and advanced democracies.
